Aerial Drone Notification System
Department of Computer Science, UNR
CS 426: Senior Projects, Spring 2018

Project Brief

Most aerial drone systems have little concern for the spatial operating conditions since they are used for outdoor activities, but spatial constraints are placed on indoor aerial drones that have limited mobility when performing similar tasks or drones that must maneuver around objects while in operation. Regarding these types of issues, the senior project that Team #23 will develop involves working with the Autonomous Robots Lab, ARL, in developing a notification system for an aerial drone that operates near obstacles such as walls and other stationary objects while indoors, in mines, or areas where space may be limited. Additionally, pertinent operational data will be monitored to assist the researchers in the ARL by monitoring aerial drone battery consumption, critical proximity surveillance, and aerial drone orientation.

Battery levels will be monitored with on-board sensors used to gauge the battery's operating current and voltage levels in real-time. It is imperative that the battery's voltage level does not fall below a certain threshold ensuring the preservation of the battery prolonging the battery's life. The notification system will monitor the operating current and voltage levels and alert the user whenever the battery is near or at the threshold set by the user. Additionally, a proximity warning system using multiple Time of Flight, ToF, sensors will monitor the drone's distance between objects such as boundary walls. Moreover, the proximity system will monitor the drone's altitude and alert the user if the drone is close to the ground while in autonomous operation.

For each critical event, the on-board sensors will relay the information wirelessly via WiFi to a base station that acts as an intermediary between the notification system and the user. The base station will gather the data and will be equipped with auditory and visual alerts for each critical event. Furthermore, the base station will have options to reset the alert system whenever the user deems it necessary. Progress made thus far includes increasing the polling rate for the sensors to ensure a proximity warning occurs as quickly as possible. The proximity sensors are polling at 60Hz which is optimal for applicative purposes. Additionally, wireless transmission has been implemented and transmission rates for data transfer has been optimized at 2000 bits per second. The wireless transmission on the receiver end has also been devised to parse the data for each transmission received to detect the sensor that triggers any proximity warnings.
